What Is a UPI QR Code and How Does It Work?

A UPI QR code is a scannable image that contains your UPI payment address (also called a VPA or Virtual Payment Address). When a customer scans the QR code with any UPI app, your payment details are automatically filled in, and they can send money to you instantly.

Behind the scenes, every UPI QR code encodes a simple string in this format:

UPI deep link format: upi://pay?pa=yourname@upi&pn=Your%20Name&am=100&cu=INR&tn=Payment%20for%20order

Where:

  • pa = your UPI ID (payment address), e.g., 9876543210@ybl or yourname@okicici
  • pn = your display name (shown to the customer)
  • am = payment amount (optional — can be fixed or left blank for customers to enter)
  • cu = currency (always INR for Indian payments)
  • tn = transaction note / description (optional)

This is a universal standard defined by NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India), which means any UPI QR code works with every UPI-enabled app. Your customer can use GPay even if you use PhonePe — it does not matter.

Best Practices for UPI QR Codes at Your Shop

  1. Print large and clear. Your QR code should be at least 5 cm x 5 cm (2 inches) for reliable scanning. Bigger is better, especially in low-light conditions. A4 size standees work great at counters.
  2. Laminate or protect from damage. A damaged QR code will not scan. Use lamination or place it behind clear glass or plastic to protect it from water, dust, and wear.
  3. Add your business name above the QR code. Print your shop or business name prominently above the QR code so customers know they are paying the right person.
  4. Include “Scan to Pay” text. Not all customers are familiar with QR payments. A simple “Scan to Pay with any UPI App” label helps.
  5. Test before printing. Always scan your generated QR code with your own phone to verify the UPI ID, name, and amount are correct before printing multiple copies.
  6. Create separate QR codes for different amounts. If you sell fixed-price items (like a Rs 100 meal or Rs 500 service), create separate QR codes with pre-set amounts for faster checkout.

Does a UPI QR code work with all payment apps?

Yes. UPI QR codes follow a universal standard by NPCI. Any QR code with a valid UPI ID works with Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, Amazon Pay, BHIM, CRED, and all bank UPI apps. The customer's app does not need to match yours.

Can I create a UPI QR code with a fixed amount?

Yes. Free Toolbox's QR Code Generator lets you set a fixed payment amount. When scanned, the amount is pre-filled in the customer's UPI app. This is great for fixed-price items or subscription payments. You can also leave the amount blank for flexible payments.

Is there any charge or transaction fee for using this QR code?

Free Toolbox charges nothing for generating the QR code. There are no transaction fees from our side. Standard UPI transactions (under Rs 2,000) typically have zero merchant charges as per NPCI guidelines. Your bank's normal UPI policies apply.

What is the difference between a bank QR code and a self-generated QR code?

Both work identically for receiving payments. Bank-issued QR codes come with merchant registration, transaction dashboards, and sometimes MDR charges. Self-generated QR codes from Free Toolbox link to your personal UPI ID, have no fees, require no registration, and are ready in 2 minutes.
