Old vs New Tax Regime FY 2025-26 — Which One Saves You More? Complete Guide

Every year, lakhs of Indian taxpayers face the same question: should I stick with the Old Tax Regime or switch to the New Tax Regime? With the changes introduced in Budget 2024, the answer has shifted significantly. The New Regime has become more attractive than ever, but the Old Regime still wins for taxpayers who maximize their deductions.

In this comprehensive guide, we break down the tax slabs for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27), compare both regimes side by side, show you the exact break-even point, and walk through real salary examples at Rs 8 lakh, Rs 12 lakh, and Rs 20 lakh. By the end, you will know exactly which regime saves you the most money.

What Changed in Budget 2024 for FY 2025-26?

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman made several changes in Union Budget 2024 to make the New Tax Regime more attractive:

  • Standard deduction increased from Rs 50,000 to Rs 75,000 under the New Regime
  • Tax slabs revised — the 5% slab now extends to Rs 4 lakh (from Rs 3 lakh above the exemption)
  • Family pension deduction raised from Rs 15,000 to Rs 25,000 under the New Regime
  • Employer NPS deduction limit raised from 10% to 14% of salary under the New Regime
  • The New Regime remains the default regime — you must explicitly opt out to use the Old Regime

The Old Regime slabs remain unchanged from previous years.

New Tax Regime Slabs for FY 2025-26

Under the New Regime, you get a standard deduction of Rs 75,000 (for salaried individuals) but cannot claim most other deductions like 80C, 80D, HRA, or LTA.

Section 87A Rebate: If your taxable income is Rs 7 lakh or below under the New Regime, you get a full rebate and pay zero tax. For salaried individuals, this means income up to Rs 7.75 lakh (7L + 75K standard deduction) is effectively tax-free.

Old Tax Regime Slabs for FY 2025-26

The Old Regime offers a standard deduction of Rs 50,000 plus access to all deductions — 80C (Rs 1.5L), 80D (Rs 25K-1L), HRA, LTA, NPS (80CCD), home loan interest, and more.

When Is the Old Regime Better?

The Old Regime wins when you have high deductions. It is typically better if:

  • You max out Section 80C (Rs 1,50,000) — PPF, ELSS, EPF, LIC, NSC, etc.
  • You claim HRA exemption (especially in metro cities where rent is high)
  • You pay health insurance premiums (Section 80D — up to Rs 25,000 for self, Rs 50,000 for senior parents)
  • You contribute to NPS (additional Rs 50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B))
  • You have a home loan (Section 24b — up to Rs 2,00,000 interest deduction)
  • Your total deductions exceed the break-even point (see below)

When Is the New Regime Better?

The New Regime wins when you have low or no deductions. It is typically better if:

  • You do not invest in 80C instruments or do not claim the full Rs 1.5 lakh
  • You live in your own house or with parents (no HRA to claim)
  • You do not have health insurance or your employer covers it
  • You are a young professional who prefers higher take-home over locked-in investments
  • Your total deductions are below the break-even threshold

Break-Even Analysis: How Much Deduction Makes Old Regime Better?

The key question is: at what level of deductions does the Old Regime start saving more than the New Regime?

Rule of thumb: For most salaried individuals, the Old Regime becomes better when your total additional deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + NPS + Home Loan Interest) exceed approximately Rs 3.75 lakh to Rs 4.25 lakh, depending on your income level. The higher your income, the higher the break-even point shifts slightly.

Here is a simplified break-even by income level:

Gross Salary Break-Even Deductions (approx.) Recommendation
Up to Rs 7.75L N/A New Regime (zero tax with rebate)
Rs 8L – 10L Rs 2.5L – 3L Old Regime if deductions > Rs 2.5-3L
Rs 10L – 15L Rs 3.5L – 4L Old Regime if deductions > Rs 3.5-4L
Rs 15L – 20L Rs 4L – 4.5L Old Regime if deductions > Rs 4-4.5L
Rs 20L+ Rs 4.25L – 5L Old Regime if deductions > Rs 4.25-5L

Scenario: Young professional, limited deductions

Example 2: Salary of Rs 12,00,000

Scenario: Mid-career professional with moderate deductions

Gross Salary: Rs 12,00,000

Deductions (Old Regime): 80C: Rs 1,50,000 + 80D: Rs 25,000 + HRA: Rs 1,20,000 + Std Deduction: Rs 50,000 = Rs 3,45,000

Tax under Old Regime: Taxable income = Rs 8,55,000. Tax = Rs 12,500 + Rs 71,000 = Rs 83,500 + 4% cess = Rs 86,840

Tax under New Regime: Taxable income = Rs 11,25,000 (after Rs 75K std deduction). Tax = Rs 20,000 + Rs 30,000 + Rs 18,750 = Rs 68,750 + 4% cess = Rs 71,500

New Regime saves Rs 15,340

Example 3: Salary of Rs 20,00,000

Scenario: Senior professional maximizing all deductions

Gross Salary: Rs 20,00,000

Deductions (Old Regime): 80C: Rs 1,50,000 + 80D: Rs 50,000 + HRA: Rs 2,40,000 + NPS 80CCD(1B): Rs 50,000 + Home Loan 24b: Rs 2,00,000 + Std Deduction: Rs 50,000 = Rs 6,40,000

Tax under Old Regime: Taxable income = Rs 13,60,000. Tax = Rs 12,500 + Rs 1,00,000 + Rs 1,08,000 = Rs 2,20,500 + 4% cess = Rs 2,29,320

Tax under New Regime: Taxable income = Rs 19,25,000. Tax = Rs 20,000 + Rs 30,000 + Rs 30,000 + Rs 60,000 + Rs 1,27,500 = Rs 2,67,500 + 4% cess = Rs 2,78,200

Old Regime saves Rs 48,880

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the default tax regime for FY 2025-26?

The New Tax Regime is the default from FY 2023-24 onwards. If you do not explicitly opt for the Old Regime when filing your ITR, you will be taxed under the New Regime automatically.

Can I claim Section 80C under the New Regime?

No. Under the New Regime, most deductions are not available, including 80C, 80D, HRA, and LTA. You only get a standard deduction of Rs 75,000 and employer NPS contribution under 80CCD(2).

Which regime is better for a salary of Rs 12 lakhs?

It depends on your deductions. If your total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + NPS) exceed roughly Rs 3.5-4 lakhs, the Old Regime may be better. Otherwise, the New Regime wins. Use ToolBox India's Tax Calculator for an exact comparison.

Can I switch between regimes every year?

Yes, salaried individuals can switch between Old and New regime every year at the time of filing their ITR. Business or professional income earners have some restrictions on switching back.

Is income up to Rs 7 lakh tax-free under the New Regime?

Effectively yes. The Section 87A rebate makes income up to Rs 7 lakh tax-free under the New Regime. For salaried individuals, adding the Rs 75K standard deduction means income up to Rs 7.75 lakh is tax-free.

What changed in Budget 2024 for the New Regime?

Standard deduction increased from Rs 50,000 to Rs 75,000. Tax slabs were revised with the 5% bracket expanding. Employer NPS contribution limit was raised from 10% to 14% of salary. Family pension deduction went up to Rs 25,000.
