Frequently Asked Questions

What calculation modes are available?

Six modes: SIP (monthly investment), Lumpsum (one-time investment), Step-Up SIP (annual increment to SIP amount), SWP (systematic withdrawal plan for drawing from your corpus), Goal-Based (reverse calculation to find the SIP needed for a target amount), and SIP vs Lumpsum (side-by-side comparison of both strategies). Each mode has dedicated input fields and provides detailed results with charts.

Does it account for taxes?

Yes! The calculator applies Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax at 12.5% on equity mutual fund gains exceeding Rs 1.25 lakh per financial year, as per Budget 2024 rules. This gives you a more realistic picture of your actual returns after tax. Most other SIP calculators do not factor in LTCG, which can lead to overestimation of your final corpus.

What does inflation-adjusted value mean?

It converts your future investment value into today's purchasing power using 6% average inflation. For example, Rs 1 crore in 15 years equals approximately Rs 41.7 lakh in today's rupees. This is crucial because a corpus that looks large in nominal terms may not be as impressive when you account for rising costs. The inflation-adjusted value helps you set realistic financial goals.

What interest rate should I use?

Use 12-15% for equity mutual funds (large cap: 10-13%, mid cap: 13-16%, small cap: 14-18%), 7-8% for debt funds and fixed income, and 10-12% for balanced or hybrid funds. These are long-term historical averages for Indian mutual funds. For conservative and realistic planning, use the lower end of each range — 10-12% for equity is a prudent assumption for long-term projections.

Is LTCG tax included?

Yes, the calculator applies 12.5% LTCG on equity gains above Rs 1.25 lakh per financial year as per Budget 2024 rules. This gives you an after-tax view of your returns, which is essential for accurate financial planning. The tax calculation is applied to your final corpus to show the real amount you take home after taxation.

What is a good monthly SIP amount for beginners?

Beginners can start with as little as Rs 500 per month — many AMCs in India accept SIPs starting from Rs 100 or Rs 500. A commonly recommended approach is to invest 20-30% of your monthly take-home income. For example, if your salary is Rs 30,000/month, starting with Rs 5,000-7,000 in SIP is a reasonable target. The key is to start early and increase your SIP amount with salary hikes using step-up SIP. Even Rs 5,000/month at 12% annual returns for 20 years grows to approximately Rs 50 lakh through the power of compounding.

How does SIP compare to lumpsum investing?

SIP spreads your investment over time through monthly instalments, automatically averaging out market highs and lows — this is called rupee cost averaging. Lumpsum invests the entire amount at once and benefits more when markets are trending upward. Historically, lumpsum has given slightly higher returns in a sustained bull market, but SIP significantly reduces timing risk and is ideal for salaried individuals investing from monthly income. Use our dedicated SIP vs Lumpsum comparison mode to see the exact difference with your numbers, expected returns, and time horizon.

What returns can I expect from SIP?

Historical long-term returns for Indian equity mutual funds (10+ year CAGR): Large Cap funds average 10-13%, Mid Cap funds 13-16%, Small Cap funds 14-18%, and Flexi/Multi Cap funds 12-15%. Debt/bond funds return 6-8%. Index funds tracking Nifty 50 have delivered approximately 12% over 15+ years. These are historical averages and actual returns vary by market conditions, fund selection, and investment timing. For conservative financial planning, use 10-12% for equity SIPs.

Is SIP better than FD?

Over the long term (7+ years), equity SIP has historically outperformed bank FDs significantly. FDs currently offer 6-7.5% returns (pre-tax, and FD interest is fully taxable at your income slab rate), while equity mutual fund SIPs have delivered 12-15% CAGR over 10+ year periods with more tax-efficient treatment (LTCG at 12.5% with Rs 1.25L annual exemption). However, FDs offer guaranteed, predictable returns and are ideal for short-term goals, emergency funds, and risk-averse investors. SIP carries market risk but offers higher growth potential. For goals 5+ years away, SIP in equity mutual funds is generally the better choice for wealth building.

How is LTCG tax calculated on SIP?

In SIP, each monthly instalment buys mutual fund units at different NAVs. When you redeem, each lot of units is tracked individually. Units held for more than 12 months qualify as long-term and attract LTCG tax. LTCG on equity mutual funds is taxed at 12.5% on gains exceeding Rs 1.25 lakh per financial year (as per Budget 2024). For example, if your total long-term gains in a year are Rs 2 lakh, tax applies only on Rs 75,000 (Rs 2L minus Rs 1.25L exemption) at 12.5% = Rs 9,375. Our calculator automatically factors this LTCG calculation into your projected returns.

Can I increase SIP amount yearly?

Yes! This is called Step-Up SIP or Top-Up SIP. You instruct your mutual fund house to increase your monthly SIP by a fixed percentage (typically 10-15%) or a fixed amount each year, usually aligned with your annual salary increment. Step-Up SIP dramatically accelerates wealth creation compared to a flat SIP. For example, Rs 10,000/month SIP with 10% annual step-up at 12% returns for 20 years grows to approximately Rs 1.2 crore, compared to Rs 1 crore with a flat Rs 10,000 SIP. Use our Step-Up SIP mode to see the exact impact of yearly increases on your final corpus.
