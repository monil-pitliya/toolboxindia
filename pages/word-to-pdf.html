The converter handles typical Word documents up to 50MB. Since everything runs in your browser, the limit depends on your device's available memory. Most standard documents, including multi-page reports, theses with embedded images, and manuscripts, convert without any issues on modern devices.

Is a watermark added?

No, absolutely not. Your converted PDF is completely watermark-free, clean, and professional. Free Toolbox never adds branding or watermarks to your output files. Can I convert .doc files?

The converter works best with .docx format (Word 2007 and later). If you have an older .doc file, save it as .docx first in Microsoft Word, LibreOffice Writer, or Google Docs, then convert it here for best results. Most modern Word documents are already in .docx format.

Does it support Hindi text?

Yes! The converter fully supports Hindi (Devanagari script), Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Bengali, Gujarati, Marathi, Punjabi, and all other Indian languages that use Unicode. If your Word document uses standard Unicode fonts like Mangal, Noto Sans Devanagari, Kruti Dev (Unicode version), or similar, the text will render correctly in the PDF output. This is essential for students and professionals who write documents in Indian languages.

The converter processes one document at a time for the best possible conversion quality and formatting accuracy. Why is the formatting different in the PDF?

Minor formatting differences can occur due to font availability. If your Word document uses custom or non-standard fonts not available in the browser, the converter substitutes similar fonts, which may cause slight layout shifts. For best results: (1) Use standard fonts like Arial, Times New Roman, Calibri, or Google Fonts; (2) Embed fonts in your Word document before converting; (3) Avoid complex features like macros, ActiveX controls, and some advanced table layouts that may render slightly differently.

Is it safe for confidential documents?

Yes, 100% safe. Your Word document is processed entirely in your browser using JavaScript — it is never uploaded to any server. No one, not even Free Toolbox, can access your files.
