JPG vs PNG: Which Format Should You Choose?

JPG (JPEG) uses lossy compression, producing smaller files ideal for photographs, general sharing on WhatsApp or email, and web use. A typical PDF page converts to a 200-500 KB JPG file at good quality.

PNG uses lossless compression, preserving every detail. Choose PNG for text-heavy documents, technical diagrams, screenshots, or when you need transparency support. PNG files are typically 2-5x larger than JPG.
