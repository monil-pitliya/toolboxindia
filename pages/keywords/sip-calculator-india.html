Understanding the SIP Formula

The Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) formula calculates compound growth on each monthly installment separately. The formula is: M = P x ({[1 + r]^n - 1} / r) x (1 + r), where M is the maturity amount, P is the monthly SIP amount, r is the monthly rate of return (annual rate divided by 12), and n is the total number of installments.

For example, a Rs 10,000 monthly SIP at 12% annual returns over 20 years would grow to approximately Rs 99.9 lakh, with only Rs 24 lakh invested and Rs 75.9 lakh earned as returns. That is the power of compounding.

LTCG Tax on Mutual Fund Returns

As per the Union Budget 2024, equity mutual fund gains above Rs 1.25 lakh per financial year are taxed at 12.5% as Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) if held for more than 12 months. Short-term gains (under 12 months) are taxed at 20%. Our calculator automatically deducts applicable LTCG tax to show you realistic post-tax returns.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is SIP return calculated?

SIP returns use the compound interest formula: M = P x ({[1 + r]^n - 1} / r) x (1 + r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly rate of return (annual rate / 12), and n is total installments. Each installment earns compound interest from its investment date.

What is a step-up SIP calculator?

A step-up SIP calculator accounts for annual increases in your SIP amount, typically 5-10% per year. As your income grows, you increase your monthly investment. This mirrors real-life behavior and shows significantly higher maturity values compared to a fixed SIP.

How much LTCG tax do I pay on SIP returns?

For equity mutual funds in India, LTCG above Rs 1.25 lakh per financial year is taxed at 12.5% (Budget 2024). Short-term gains (under 1 year) are taxed at 20%. Our calculator factors in LTCG to show actual post-tax returns.

What is a good expected return rate for SIP in India?

Indian equity mutual funds have historically delivered 12-15% annualized returns over 10+ years. Large cap funds average around 12%, mid cap around 14%, and small cap around 15-18%. Use 10-12% for conservative estimates. Debt funds deliver 6-8%.

How does inflation affect my SIP returns?

Inflation reduces purchasing power. At 6% inflation, Rs 1 crore after 20 years has the purchasing power of only Rs 31 lakh today. Our inflation-adjusted mode shows the real value of your SIP maturity amount.

What is the minimum SIP amount in India?

Most AMCs allow SIPs from Rs 500/month. Some like SBI, HDFC, and ICICI Prudential offer Rs 100 SIPs. Use our calculator to see how even small monthly investments grow significantly over 15-20 years through compounding.

SIP vs Lump Sum: Which is better?

SIP is better for salaried individuals, providing rupee cost averaging and disciplined investing. Lump sum can outperform in a rising market. Use our SIP vs Lump Sum comparison mode to see which works better for your scenario.

How much SIP is needed to get Rs 1 crore?

At 12% annual returns: Rs 5,000/month for 25 years, Rs 10,000/month for 20 years, or Rs 26,000/month for 15 years accumulates approximately Rs 1 crore. Use our goal-based calculator for your exact target.
