Key Budget 2025 Changes

  • Zero tax up to Rs 12 lakh — under new regime, taxable income up to Rs 12 lakh gets full Section 87A rebate. For salaried (with Rs 75K standard deduction), this means zero tax on salary up to Rs 12,75,000
  • Standard deduction Rs 75,000 — increased from Rs 50,000 under new regime, reducing taxable income for all salaried individuals
  • Revised slab structure — uniform Rs 4 lakh slab width with rates from 5% to 30%, providing more benefit at higher income levels
  • New regime is default — the new tax regime continues as the default. You must explicitly opt for old regime if preferred
  • NPS deduction in new regime — employer NPS contribution up to 14% of salary is deductible under the new regime (Section 80CCD(2))
  • TDS thresholds relaxed — higher thresholds for TDS on various incomes, reducing compliance burden

New Regime vs Old Regime — When to Choose Which?

  • Choose NEW regime if: Your total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + home loan + others) are less than Rs 3-4 lakh, or if you prefer simplicity with lower tax rates
  • Choose OLD regime if: You have heavy deductions — high HRA in metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore), home loan interest exceeding Rs 2 lakh, significant 80C investments (Rs 1.5L), medical insurance premiums, NPS contributions, LTA claims
  • Income below Rs 12.75 lakh (salaried)? — New regime is almost always better (zero tax with Section 87A rebate)
  • Income Rs 15-25 lakh range? — Compare both using the calculator. This is where the decision depends heavily on your specific deduction profile
  • Income above Rs 25 lakh? — Old regime may save more if you have maximum deductions (Rs 4-5 lakh total). Otherwise, new regime's lower rates win

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the new income tax slabs for FY 2025-26?

Under the new tax regime for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) as per Budget 2025: 0-4 lakh: Nil, 4-8 lakh: 5%, 8-12 lakh: 10%, 12-16 lakh: 15%, 16-20 lakh: 20%, 20-24 lakh: 25%, Above 24 lakh: 30%. Plus 4% health and education cess on total tax.

What is the standard deduction for FY 2025-26?

The standard deduction is Rs 75,000 under the new tax regime (increased from Rs 50,000 in the previous year). Under the old tax regime, it remains at Rs 50,000. This is automatically deducted from salary income before calculating tax.

What is the Section 87A rebate for FY 2025-26?

Under the new regime, if your taxable income (after standard deduction) is up to Rs 12 lakh, you get a full tax rebate under Section 87A of up to Rs 60,000. This means salaried individuals earning up to Rs 12,75,000 pay zero income tax. Under the old regime, the rebate is Rs 12,500 for income up to Rs 5 lakh.

Which is better — new regime or old regime?

The new regime is generally better if your total deductions are less than Rs 3-4 lakh. The old regime may save more if you have heavy deductions — high HRA, home loan interest, large 80C investments, and NPS contributions. Use Free Toolbox's calculator to compare both instantly with your actual numbers.

Is the new tax regime the default?

Yes, the new tax regime has been the default since FY 2023-24. If you want to use the old regime, you must explicitly opt for it while filing your ITR. Salaried employees can also inform their employer to deduct TDS under the old regime.

How is tax calculated on Rs 12 lakh salary?

For Rs 12 lakh salary under new regime FY 2025-26: Gross salary Rs 12,00,000 minus standard deduction Rs 75,000 = taxable income Rs 11,25,000. Since taxable income is under Rs 12 lakh, the entire tax is rebated under Section 87A. Net tax payable: Rs 0 (zero).

Do I need to pay tax if my income is Rs 15 lakh?

Yes. For Rs 15 lakh salary under new regime: After Rs 75,000 standard deduction, taxable income is Rs 14,25,000. Tax: Nil on first 4L + Rs 20,000 (5% on 4-8L) + Rs 40,000 (10% on 8-12L) + Rs 33,750 (15% on 12-14.25L) = Rs 93,750 + 4% cess = approximately Rs 97,500.

Can I switch between old and new regime every year?

Salaried individuals (without business income) can switch between old and new regime every financial year while filing their ITR. Business owners with business income can switch from new to old regime only once, and that choice is generally final. Consult a chartered accountant for business income cases.
