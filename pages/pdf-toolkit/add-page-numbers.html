Frequently Asked Questions

What page number positions are available?

Six positions are available, covering all standard document layouts: Top-Left, Top-Center, Top-Right, Bottom-Left, Bottom-Center, and Bottom-Right. Bottom-Center is the most common choice for reports and essays, while Top-Right is popular for formal documents and legal submissions.

What page number formats can I choose?

Four formats are available: Plain (1, 2, 3) for simple numbering, Dashed (-1-, -2-, -3-) for a decorative style, Of-Total (1 of 10, 2 of 10) to show total page count, and Page Label (Page 1, Page 2) for formal documents. Each format gives your document a professional touch suited to its purpose.

Can I start page numbering from a specific page?

Yes. You can set the starting page number to any value. If your document continues from another file, start numbering from page 15 or any other number. You can also choose which page the numbering begins on, allowing you to skip cover pages, title pages, or the table of contents.

Can I change the font size of page numbers?

Yes, the tool offers multiple font size options. Choose a smaller size for subtle, footnote-style numbering or a larger size for prominent page identification. The font size options are designed to work well with standard document layouts and page sizes.
