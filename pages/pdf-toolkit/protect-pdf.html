Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between user password and owner password?

The user password (also called the open password) is required to open and view the PDF — without it, the document is completely inaccessible. The owner password (also called the permissions password) restricts specific actions like editing, copying text, content extraction, and printing. You can set both for maximum security, or use just one depending on your needs.

What encryption method is used?

The tool uses AES (Advanced Encryption Standard), the gold standard of encryption used by governments, banks, and military organizations worldwide. AES encryption is considered virtually unbreakable with current computing technology when paired with a strong password. Your documents are as secure as online banking transactions.

Can I prevent people from editing or copying my PDF?

Yes. By setting an owner password, you can restrict modification, text copying, content extraction, and printing. Recipients can view the document in a PDF reader but cannot make changes, copy text to clipboard, or print it without knowing the owner password.

Is my password stored anywhere?

Absolutely not. Your password is never stored, transmitted, logged, or recorded anywhere. The entire encryption process happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. No data — not the PDF file, not the password, not even metadata — ever leaves your device. This is the most private way to encrypt a PDF.

What if I forget the password?

Since encryption happens entirely in your browser and we never store or see your password, there is no password recovery option. We strongly recommend saving your password in a trusted password manager (like Bitwarden, 1Password, or KeePass) and always keeping a copy of the original unprotected PDF file as a backup.
