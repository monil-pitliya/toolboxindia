Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert multiple images into one PDF?

Yes! Upload multiple JPG, PNG, or WebP images, drag to reorder them, choose page size (A4, Letter, Legal) and orientation (portrait or landscape), then download a single merged PDF. There is no limit on the number of images — it depends on your device's memory. This makes it ideal for combining scanned documents, photo collections, and multi-page assignments into one file.

What page sizes are supported?

A4 (210 x 297 mm), Letter (8.5 x 11 inches), Legal (8.5 x 14 inches), and custom sizes are all supported. A4 is the most common for international documents and Indian official use, while Letter is standard in the US. Legal is available for legal-size documents, and custom lets you set exact dimensions for special requirements.

Can I choose portrait or landscape?

Yes, you can select portrait or landscape orientation before converting. Choose landscape for wide images like panoramas, certificates, or screenshots, and portrait for standard document-style layouts like letters and ID card copies. The orientation applies to all pages in the generated PDF.

How to convert multiple images to one PDF?

Simply upload all your images at once using drag-and-drop or the file picker. You can select multiple files by holding Ctrl (or Cmd on Mac). Once uploaded, drag to reorder them in your preferred sequence, choose your page size and orientation, then click Convert. All images will be merged into a single PDF with each image on its own page. This works great for combining scanned documents, photographs, and assignment pages.

Can I convert HEIC photos from iPhone?

HEIC is the default photo format on iPhones running iOS 11 and later. Most modern browsers on iOS and macOS can handle HEIC files natively. For the best compatibility, you can share photos from your iPhone directly through Safari, or use the built-in iOS share option to convert to JPG first. If you are on Windows or Android, consider converting HEIC to JPG before uploading. Our tool works seamlessly with JPG, PNG, and WebP images from any device.

What is the best page size for documents?

For Indian documents like Aadhaar copies, PAN cards, mark sheets, college certificates, and official letters, A4 (210 x 297 mm) is the standard and universally accepted format. For US-style documents, use Letter (8.5 x 11 inches). Legal size (8.5 x 14 inches) is best for legal agreements and affidavits. When in doubt, choose A4 as it is the most widely accepted format in India and internationally.
